To confirm computations made by program Distance, below are components from a line transect distance sampling survey and analysis needed to produce a density estimate. Use these components to answer the following questions, along with these formulas from the lecture in Chapter 1.

\[ \hat{D} = \frac{n}{2wL \hat{P}_a} \hspace{2cm} \hat{N} = \frac{nA}{2wL \hat{P}_a} \]

The truncation distance (\(w\)) for the survey was 1.94. From the fitting of a detection function, we also know that the probability of detection of animals within the covered area \((\hat{P}_a)\) is 0.5408.
